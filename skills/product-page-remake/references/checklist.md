# Product Page Remake Checklist

Use this when the task requires a full teardown, page rewrite, or handoff to design/development.

## Intake

- Reference: URL, screenshots, saved HTML, copied content, or marketplace listing.
- User product: name, SKU, price, offer, audience, specs, differentiators, photos, proof, shipping/returns.
- Platform: Shopify, WooCommerce, custom site, Amazon, TikTok Shop, Douyin, Taobao/Tmall, JD, or other marketplace.
- Deliverable: teardown, copy deck, visual plan, HTML prototype, platform section, product-image shot list, or upload-ready content.
- Constraints: brand tone, forbidden claims, required disclaimers, localization, language, legal/compliance limits.

## Reference Teardown Fields

Capture these as observations, not as content to copy:

- Above-the-fold promise and CTA.
- Product positioning and target customer.
- Image/media sequence and what each asset proves.
- Benefit hierarchy and emotional hooks.
- Feature/spec explanation order.
- Social proof: reviews, UGC, press, expert proof, before/after, usage scenes.
- Risk reducers: guarantees, returns, shipping, payment security, certifications.
- Objection handling: fit, size, durability, material, care, safety, price, comparison.
- Scarcity or urgency mechanics.
- Cross-sell, bundle, and price framing.
- FAQ and post-purchase reassurance.
- Mobile behavior: sticky CTA, accordions, image ratio, text density.

## Originality Transform

For every strong competitor element, transform at least two layers:

- Change the specific wording and claims.
- Change the proof source to user-owned proof or a placeholder request.
- Change the visual composition, crop, scene, or sequence.
- Change the information architecture when the user's buying argument is different.
- Change examples and metaphors to match the user's audience.

Do not use competitor reviews, testimonials, logos, certificates, original photos, illustrations, icons, code, or exact layout measurements unless the user owns or licenses them.

## Recommended Page Sections

Use only the sections that fit the product:

1. Hero: product identity, core promise, price/offer, CTA, strongest visual.
2. Problem or desire: why the product matters now.
3. Core benefits: 3-5 benefit blocks grounded in product facts.
4. Proof media: owned photos, usage scenes, UGC placeholders, demo clips.
5. Feature/spec detail: material, dimensions, compatibility, ingredients, care, package contents.
6. Comparison: against alternatives or old way, with fair claims.
7. Trust: reviews, guarantees, shipping, return policy, payment security.
8. Offer stack: bundle, promotion, savings, bonuses.
9. FAQ: objections that would block purchase.
10. Final CTA: concise reminder and purchase action.

## Copy Quality Checks

- Lead with buyer outcome, not internal product features.
- Use concrete nouns and measurable details where available.
- Avoid generic phrases like "high quality", "perfect choice", or "must-have" unless supported by specifics.
- Keep mobile paragraphs short.
- Repeat CTA after major persuasion blocks.
- Mark unsupported claims as `[proof needed]`.
- Match platform restrictions for health, beauty, finance, child products, and regulated categories.

## Coded Prototype Checks

- Use responsive constraints for media, sticky bars, accordions, and CTAs.
- Use user-owned images or placeholders with clear filenames.
- Avoid embedding competitor tracking scripts, third-party widgets, or copied HTML/CSS.
- Test desktop and mobile screenshots for overlapping text and clipped buttons.
- Keep page sections unframed unless the design calls for repeated product cards or testimonials.
